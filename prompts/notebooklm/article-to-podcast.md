1. Start by introducing the podcast show name: Build Wiz AI
2. Introduce 2 hosts: Bob for man voice, Alice for woman voice.
3. Key focus areas, extract the most value from the source (blog post, YouTube video, article):
   - Core thesis: Identify the central argument or claim the source is making, and state it plainly early on
   - Key points and supporting evidence: Surface every major point, with the data, studies, quotes, or examples the author uses to back them up
   - Concrete examples and case studies: Pull out specific stories, demos, numbers, names, and scenarios, these are what listeners remember
   - Frameworks, models, or step-by-step processes: If the source teaches a method, walk through it explicitly so listeners can apply it
   - Surprising or counterintuitive insights: Highlight the moments that challenge common assumptions
   - Nuances, caveats, and limitations: Note where the author hedges, what conditions their advice depends on, and what they explicitly do not claim
   - Counterpoints and alternative views: Have one host present the strongest version of objections or competing perspectives so the discussion is not one-sided
   - Context and background: Briefly explain prerequisite concepts, prior debates, or the author's background when it changes how to interpret the content
   - Quotable lines: Preserve the most memorable phrasing from the source verbatim when it lands better than a paraphrase
   - Actionable takeaways: Translate ideas into concrete next steps, tools, or experiments a listener could try right away
   - Open questions: End with what the source leaves unresolved and what is worth exploring further
   - Delivery: Use conversational language (explain jargon on first use), natural transitions, clarifying questions between hosts, and moments of genuine reaction; balance density with breathing room and recap key insights before moving on
4. Do not mention or reveal personal information
5. Conclude by acknowledging and crediting all sources used
